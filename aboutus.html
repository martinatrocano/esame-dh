About Us

The photos of artworks collected by Carlo Volpe are part of the Fototeca Supino which was founded by scholar Igino Benvenuto Supino in collaboration with the Istituto di Storia dell’Arte of the University of Bologna. Essential documents and tools for the art history’s research, the archive now counts 71000 photographs divided into 4 sections: Fototeca Supino, Archivio Croci, Fototeca Volpe and Miscellanea Istituto di Storia dell'Arte. Due to complexity and breadth of the collection, we selected a specific topic investigated by Carlo Volpe with his photographic collection: the Bolognese artistic production in the Trecento. As a result, our digitalization campaign focused on cataloguing six folders in the collection related to that topic. We planned our project in stages, dividing ourselves into working teams based on the tasks we needed to complete: - Researching the bibliography using volumes and books in the Supino Library and in the Fondazione Zeri; - Managing the digitization process using a specific scanning machine; - Organizing the cataloguing process referring to specific standards with the platform Archiui; - Conceiving the curatorial project for the digital exhibition; - Organizing a communication campaign on social media for monitoring our work. Conservation The primary source for our project were the real photos that are conserved and preserved in the archive respecting their nature of fragile objects. The pictures were originally inventoried and catalogued with the insertion of a specific inventory number that reveals the photos’ current location in the Fototeca. The disposition of each photo reflects the archive's organization which we must take in mind during the digitization process. Pictures were placed in acid-free folders and boxes and kept in an air-conditioned room to maintain the proper temperature and humidity for conservation. When starting to digitize photos, we must assure that we handle these materials with care, wearing cotton gloves. Digitalization The digitization process must be very precise. Each photo was scanned by us using a specific scanning machine which creates a digital copy of the analogue photograph that must be reproduced in both its recto and verso. When the digital copy was saved in the PC, the post-production and quality control work began, adhering to a set of reference standards and parameters. The master file of the photo was saved reporting its cataloguing number and in parallel catalogued in the content management system we chose for managing the documents: Archiui. Cataloguing with Archiui Archiui is the first digital platform dedicated to historical archives. Developed by Promemoria, its main goal is to organize and enhance the historical and cultural heritage of brands, companies and institutions. The platform follows the analysis and arrangement of heritage into categories, and then develops organization systems and vocabularies tailored to each institution. Archiui gave us the possibility to arrange the inventory, in which we created the virtual versions of the physical folders; in each of them, we uploaded all the previously scanned works, in the forms of “Scheda opera” and “Scheda foto” (see Appendix 1). Within the “Scheda opera” we have placed all the entries with information regarding the work, such as author, year, location, type of work, materials used etc. While in the "Scheda foto" we have included all the entries related to the photograph itself, such as identification, type of photograph, author, year, etc. Based on collective access, Archiui allowed us to build up connections between the different items, and helped us in rendering the system of the works relations. The cataloguing part is carried out in the back end of Archiui, and it is where the digitized works took shape. In this section it is possible to browse through the catalogued collections and folders, letting oneself be inspired by the images in the archive. Social Media/Communication The Instagram page has been used to share and promote the work gathered by the students during the laboratory of Valorisation of Medieval and Modern Art. During the first part of the course, we considered international museums’ social media strategies and the latest innovations in promotion in cultural heritage since the spreading of social media. In our Instagram page, we have tried to apply some of these strategies to the project on Carlo Volpe’s archive.

The structure of the Instagram Page has been conceived with the aim to be visually coherent and pleasing for users. A common graphic and a logo have been created in order to present a unique style for the feed. As for the posts, they focused both on the content of the online exhibition on Archui and the documentation of the activities carried by the students during classes, specifically the work of digitalization of the Carlo Volpe’s archive. Interviews with students and professors were included in the feed, aiming to share diverse points of view about the importance of valorisation and digitization of cultural heritage. The instagram feed has been divided into three visual columns: the one on the right gathers has been dedicated to interviews with professors and professionals in the field of Medieval Art History. The central one has been completely dedicated to the work of digitalization of the archive, showing the process needed for the digitalization of a photo archive. At last, the one on the left has been opened to students’ sharing their experience and thoughts about the link between valorization and digitalization of the archive.

Through the Instagram stories, we tried to boost users’ curiosity by posting quizzes about works from the Carlo Volpe’s archive and photos showing the students working in class.
